Office Hours Statement

Office hours exist for ad-hoc help with course concepts, assignments, and anything else requiring my attention or support. Office hours are walk-in (no appointment needed), and are shared between sections. In order to best serve the entire class, office hours are on a first-come, first-served, rotating-help schedule. This means you can make most effective use of office hours time by arriving early with specific questions already prepared.

Examples of effective use of office hours:

  • "In yesterday's lecture, we went over an example where used a new kind of loop that we hadn't seen before. Could you explain why we would want to use this instead of the ones we've covered before while solving a real problem?"
  • "This week we've been learning about objects, and I don't think I'm all the way there on understanding why we use them. I did all the reading exercises, but I think I could benefit from another example where more than one object works together."
  • "I'm making good progress on the lab, but when I run my program the output is always off by one. Here is the section of my code that counts rows, could I walk you through what I'm doing to see if you can find the error?"

Examples of ineffective use of office hours:

  • "I missed class this week. Can you teach me everything we went over?"
    • Instead, try to read the lecture notes from the sessions you missed, do the reading activities, and come with questions about individual parts that don't make sense.
  • "I feel like I just don't get anything at all."
    • Instead, try to identify specific topics you do not understand. It can help to re-read the objectives at the top of each lecture's notes and ask: "Can I do this?"
  • "I don't know where to start on the lab."
    • Instead, try writing a plain language description and flowchart for how you would solve the lab problem as a normal human. Then identify which specific instructions you're having trouble translating into program code.

In addition to the four scheduled office hours listed above, I also reserve a few additional hours each week for "floating" meetings scheduled with students who cannot attend office hours. Please contact me by email if you wish to reserve a block of this floating time (note: this time is limited, so availability in the same week as your request is not guaranteed. Please try to make it to office hours instead if able.)
