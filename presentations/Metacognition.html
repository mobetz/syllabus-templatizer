The Metacognitive Model

What is metacognition?

Metacognition can be broken down into "cognition", which is thinking, and "meta-", which means 'about itself'. Altogether, metacognition means "thinking about thinking."

Metacognition is a popular area of research. Many psychologists have studied how we think, and more specifically, how we learn.

The Metacognitive Model

Dr. Barry Zimmerman theorized that self-regulated learning happens in a cycle of three stages:

  1. Forethought - Setting a goal, then scheduling and planning how to achieve that goal.
  2. Performance - Implementing your plan while monitoring performance.
  3. Self-Reflection - Thinking back on records of your performance and evaluating successes/failures.

Neuroplasticity

What is a neuron?

A neuron is a cell in your brain that acts as an information messenger.

These cells use a pulse of electricity (and some salts) to help your thinking jump between related ideas.

Neuroplasticity

Clusters of neurons:

  • record memories
  • process sensory inputs (like sight and hearing)
  • represent ideas and knowledge

Your brain is really just a large spiderweb of connected neurons.

When two ideas become connected in your mind, the neurons representing those ideas stretch and grow, building an electrical path between those ideas in your brain!
